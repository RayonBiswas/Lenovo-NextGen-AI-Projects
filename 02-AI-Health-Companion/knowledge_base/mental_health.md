# Mental Wellness Support

## Understanding Stress and Anxiety
Occasional stress and anxiety are normal responses to challenges. They become a concern when they're persistent, overwhelming, or interfere with daily life — affecting sleep, appetite, work, or relationships.

Common signs include:
- Constant worry or racing thoughts
- Difficulty sleeping or sleeping too much
- Irritability or feeling on edge
- Trouble concentrating
- Physical symptoms like headaches, stomach issues, or muscle tension

## Simple Coping Techniques

### Grounding Exercise (5-4-3-2-1)
Name 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste. This helps bring attention back to the present moment.

### Deep Breathing
Breathe in slowly for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale for 6-8 seconds. Repeat for a few minutes to calm the nervous system.

### Journaling
Writing down thoughts and worries can help process emotions and identify patterns over time.

### Movement
Even a short walk can reduce stress hormones and improve mood through light physical activity.

## Building Daily Mental Wellness Habits
- Maintain a regular sleep schedule
- Stay connected with friends and family
- Set small, achievable daily goals
- Limit news and social media if it increases anxiety
- Spend time on hobbies or activities that bring enjoyment

## When to Seek Professional Help
Consider reaching out to a mental health professional if:
- Feelings of sadness, anxiety, or hopelessness persist for more than two weeks
- Daily functioning (work, relationships, self-care) is significantly affected
- There are thoughts of self-harm or harming others
- Coping strategies no longer feel sufficient

Speaking with a counselor, therapist, or doctor is a sign of strength, not weakness. Many people benefit from professional support at different points in life.

## Crisis Support
If someone is in crisis or having thoughts of self-harm, they should reach out immediately to a local emergency service, a crisis helpline, or a trusted person nearby. In India, the Tele-MANAS helpline (14416) provides 24/7 mental health support.
