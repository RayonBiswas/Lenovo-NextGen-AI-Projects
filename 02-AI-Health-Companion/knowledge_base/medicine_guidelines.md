# Medicine Guidelines & Reminder Basics

## General Medication Safety
- Always take medicines as prescribed by a doctor or as per the label instructions.
- Do not double a dose if one is missed — take the next dose at its regular time unless a doctor advises otherwise.
- Store medicines in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and out of reach of children.
- Check expiry dates regularly and dispose of expired medicines safely.

## Common Over-the-Counter Medicines

### Paracetamol (Acetaminophen)
Used for fever and mild pain relief. Typical adult dose is 500-650mg every 4-6 hours, not exceeding 4 doses in 24 hours. Avoid combining with other paracetamol-containing products to prevent overdose.

### Antacids
Used for indigestion and acidity. Best taken 30 minutes after meals or as directed. Avoid prolonged daily use without medical advice.

### Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS)
Used to prevent dehydration during fever, vomiting, or diarrhea. Mix as per packet instructions with clean water and sip slowly throughout the day.

### Cough Syrups
Choose based on type of cough (dry vs. productive). Avoid giving adult cough syrups to children without pediatric guidance.

## Setting Up Medication Reminders
A good reminder routine includes:
1. **Medicine name** — what you're taking
2. **Dosage** — how much (e.g., 1 tablet, 5ml)
3. **Time(s) of day** — morning, afternoon, night, or specific times
4. **Frequency** — daily, twice daily, as needed
5. **Duration** — how many days the course should continue

Consistency matters most — taking medicines at the same time each day improves effectiveness, especially for antibiotics and chronic condition medicines (e.g., blood pressure, diabetes).

## Why Missed Doses Matter
Missing doses of antibiotics can lead to incomplete treatment and antibiotic resistance. Missing doses of chronic medication (BP, diabetes, thyroid) can cause symptom flare-ups and long-term complications. Reminder systems significantly improve adherence, especially for elderly patients and those managing multiple medications.

## Disclaimer
This information is for general awareness only. Always follow the dosage and duration prescribed by your doctor or pharmacist.
