# General Health FAQ

## Fever
A fever is usually a sign that the body is fighting an infection. Mild fever (below 100.9°F / 38.3°C) can often be managed at home with rest, fluids, and a fever-reducing medicine like paracetamol. See a doctor if fever lasts more than 3 days, exceeds 103°F (39.4°C), or comes with rash, stiff neck, or severe pain.

## Headache
Common causes include dehydration, lack of sleep, stress, eye strain, and skipped meals. Drinking water, resting in a dark room, and reducing screen time often help. Frequent or severe headaches, especially with vision changes or vomiting, need medical attention.

## Cold and Cough
Usually caused by viral infections and resolve in 7-10 days. Warm fluids, steam inhalation, honey (for adults), and rest help symptoms. Persistent cough beyond 2 weeks, blood in mucus, or breathing difficulty should be checked by a doctor.

## Body Pain and Fatigue
Often linked to viral infections, overexertion, dehydration, or poor sleep. Rest, hydration, and light stretching usually help. Body pain with high fever, swelling, or that doesn't improve in a few days warrants medical evaluation.

## Stomach Ache / Indigestion
Common causes are overeating, spicy food, stress, or mild infections. Light meals, ginger tea, and avoiding oily food can help. Severe pain, persistent vomiting, or blood in stool require urgent care.

## When to See a Doctor Immediately
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- High fever (above 103°F / 39.4°C) that doesn't reduce
- Severe or worsening pain
- Confusion, fainting, or seizures
- Symptoms lasting more than a few days without improvement
