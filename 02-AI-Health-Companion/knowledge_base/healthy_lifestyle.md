# Healthy Lifestyle Guide

## Sleep
Adults should aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night. Poor sleep is linked to weakened immunity, weight gain, mood disorders, and reduced concentration.

Tips for better sleep:
- Keep a consistent sleep and wake time, even on weekends
- Avoid screens (phone, laptop, TV) at least 30 minutes before bed
- Avoid caffeine after late afternoon
- Keep the bedroom dark, cool, and quiet

## Hydration
Most adults need around 2-3 liters of water per day, more in hot climates or with physical activity. Signs of dehydration include dark urine, fatigue, dizziness, and dry mouth.

Tips:
- Keep a water bottle within reach throughout the day
- Drink a glass of water first thing in the morning
- Increase intake during exercise, fever, or hot weather

## Diet
A balanced diet includes:
- Whole grains (rice, wheat, oats)
- Proteins (lentils, eggs, dairy, lean meat, beans)
- Fruits and vegetables (variety of colors for different nutrients)
- Healthy fats (nuts, seeds, olive oil)

Limit ultra-processed foods, excess sugar, and fried items. Eating at regular times helps maintain stable energy and digestion.

## Physical Activity
At least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week is recommended (e.g., 30 minutes, 5 days a week). This can include walking, cycling, swimming, or home workouts.

Benefits include better cardiovascular health, improved mood, weight management, and reduced risk of chronic disease.

## Stress Management
Chronic stress affects sleep, digestion, immunity, and mental health. Helpful practices include:
- Deep breathing exercises (e.g., 4-7-8 breathing)
- Short daily walks, ideally outdoors
- Journaling or talking to someone about worries
- Limiting constant notifications and screen time

## Daily Habit Checklist
- [ ] 7-9 hours of sleep
- [ ] 2-3 liters of water
- [ ] At least one serving of fruits/vegetables per meal
- [ ] 30 minutes of movement
- [ ] A few minutes of relaxation or mindfulness

## Disclaimer
These are general wellness guidelines. Individuals with existing health conditions should consult a doctor or dietitian before making major lifestyle changes.
